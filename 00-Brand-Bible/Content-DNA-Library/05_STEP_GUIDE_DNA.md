# Step Guide DNA

## Purpose
Turn a process Vikash has genuinely used or tested into instructions another person can execute.

## Best use cases
Git and repository workflows, AI-assisted build processes, content operations, research methods, and repeatable business checks.

## Recommended content pillars
Build With Vikash, AI Visibility Journal, Marketing Beyond Marketing, and AGMP With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Outcome -> prerequisites and assumptions -> ordered steps -> verification -> failure or recovery notes.

## Hook patterns
- "Here is the exact sequence I used to..."
- "Before running the first command, confirm..."

## Body development
Make the steps complete and executable. Distinguish strategic choices from execution commands. Before terminal commands, state the full working-directory path and the expected repository or environment.

## Ending patterns
End with the verification condition, known limitation, or one decision the reader must make.

## Tone
Precise, calm, practical, and honest about prerequisites.

## Evidence requirements
The workflow must have been used or tested. Label untested alternatives and environment-dependent steps.

## What to avoid
Missing assumptions, unexplained jumps, partial commands, strategy presented as a command sequence, and terminal instructions without the full working-directory path.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- A safe Git workflow for checking the worktree, staging only approved files, committing, pushing, and verifying origin.
- The tested content-production path from approved post to repository metadata.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Prerequisites and assumptions are explicit.
- [ ] Steps are ordered and executable.
- [ ] Full working-directory paths precede terminal commands.
- [ ] Verification and recovery are included.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
AI Builder for learning context; Systems Thinking for dependencies; Failure and Recovery for troubleshooting.
