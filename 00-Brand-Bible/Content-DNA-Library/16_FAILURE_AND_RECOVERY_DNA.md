# Failure and Recovery DNA

## Purpose
Explain a real failure, the response, and the resulting change without manufacturing drama or converting the incident into a heroic comeback.

## Best use cases
Broken workspaces, failed integrations, rejected assumptions, campaign review gaps, workflow errors, and product experiments.

## Recommended content pillars
Build With Vikash, Leadership With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, and AI Visibility Journal.

## Core narrative structure
Intended outcome -> actual failure -> diagnosis -> response -> verified current state -> lesson.

## Hook patterns
Open with what failed or the moment the assumption stopped working. Keep consequences proportionate.

## Body development
Show the recovery sequence, including uncertainty and incomplete results. Credit systems, tools, or people fairly and state what remains unresolved.

## Ending patterns
End with the changed safeguard, operating rule, or next test—not a triumph speech.

## Tone
Calm, accountable, specific, and useful.

## Evidence requirements
The failure, cause, action, and recovery state must be verified. If the cause is uncertain, say so.

## What to avoid
Manufactured drama, heroic comeback language, exaggerated damage, blame, fake vulnerability, and claiming full recovery when only a workaround exists.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Fixing broken workspaces and discovering further tool or permission dependencies.
- Receiving difficult feedback that marketing metrics were insufficient for discussing business ROI.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The failure and impact are accurate.
- [ ] Cause versus hypothesis is clear.
- [ ] Recovery status is not overstated.
- [ ] The post leaves a practical safeguard.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Candid Operator for trust; Systems Thinking for causes; AI Builder or Project Resume for build evidence.
