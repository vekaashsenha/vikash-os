# Customer Moment DNA

## Purpose
Use one genuine customer request, friction point, or service observation to reveal a need worth understanding without overgeneralising it.

## Best use cases
Service design, mobility, customer experience, unmet needs, operational friction, and product-discovery questions.

## Recommended content pillars
Mobility With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, Buyer Psychology With Vikash, and AI Visibility Journal.

## Core narrative structure
Specific customer moment -> need or friction -> current operating response -> wider question -> evidence or next step.

## Hook patterns
Open with the real request or moment in privacy-safe language. Avoid dramatic interpretation.

## Body development
Describe what the moment revealed and what it did not prove. Explain the process or product implication without turning one customer into a whole market.

## Ending patterns
End with the question the business should investigate or the next honest operating step.

## Tone
Attentive, respectful, practical, and customer-centred.

## Evidence requirements
The moment must be genuine. Any company response, roadmap, or implementation status must be verified.

## What to avoid
Invented customer quotations, emotional exploitation, market-size claims from one request, confidential details, and claiming a feature is confirmed when it is only being evaluated.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- The child-seat request that exposed a potentially overlooked mobility need. The canonical status is that the company is evaluating the request unless formal confirmation exists.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The customer moment happened.
- [ ] Privacy is protected.
- [ ] Signal and conclusion are separated.
- [ ] Company evaluation versus confirmation is stated accurately.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Corporate for operating context; Buyer Psychology for needs and risk; Kid Wisdom when a genuine child-related moment is essential.
