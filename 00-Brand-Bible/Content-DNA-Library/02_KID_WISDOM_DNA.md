# Kid Wisdom DNA

## Purpose
Use a genuine, privacy-safe child-related moment to reveal a simple human or customer truth. This framework was previously described as "Kid Wisdom Viral DNA"; virality is not the objective and is not part of the canonical name.

## Best use cases
Only when a real child-related incident naturally changes how Vikash sees a customer need, service, decision, or assumption.

## Recommended content pillars
Mobility With Vikash, Leadership With Vikash, and Marketing Beyond Marketing.

## Core narrative structure
Genuine moment -> simple observation or need -> adult/business assumption exposed -> practical lesson.

## Hook patterns
Open with the real request or situation without turning the child into a content device.

## Body development
Use the minimum personal detail needed. Focus on the need or insight, not the child's identity, emotion, or entertainment value.

## Ending patterns
End with the changed business question or a thoughtful customer-experience question.

## Tone
Respectful, gentle, restrained, and free of sentimentality.

## Evidence requirements
The incident must be genuine and publicly safe. Do not invent a child's words, feelings, motives, or response.

## What to avoid
Privacy breaches, emotional exploitation, cute-for-reach framing, invented quotations, identifiable family details, and claims that one moment proves a market.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- The child-seat request as a signal that a standard mobility service may overlook a specific customer need; do not add a child's quotation.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The moment is genuine and necessary to the lesson.
- [ ] Identity and privacy are protected.
- [ ] No emotion or quotation was invented.
- [ ] The child is not being used to manufacture engagement.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Customer Moment for the signal; Corporate for a safe operating response; Wrap for a simple close.
