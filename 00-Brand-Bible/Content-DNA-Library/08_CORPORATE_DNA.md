# Corporate DNA

## Purpose
Turn a real workplace or cross-functional situation into a useful business lesson while protecting people, organisations, and legitimate stakeholder concerns.

## Best use cases
Reviews, disagreements, operating handoffs, customer requests, leadership feedback, and cross-functional trade-offs.

## Recommended content pillars
Marketing Beyond Marketing, Leadership With Vikash, Mobility With Vikash, and AGMP With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Anonymised situation -> stakeholder concern -> tension or constraint -> what changed in Vikash's thinking -> business lesson.

## Hook patterns
Open with the safe fact of the review, request, disagreement, or constraint. Use role labels only when necessary.

## Body development
Represent other perspectives fairly. Remove identifying details and explain why a stakeholder's concern was legitimate, even when it challenged Vikash.

## Ending patterns
End with the operating principle, changed question, or balanced trade-off.

## Tone
Professional, fair, discreet, and free of score-settling.

## Evidence requirements
The event must be genuine and publicly defensible. Confidentiality review is mandatory; quotations require permission or approved wording.

## What to avoid
Gossip, blame, identifiable internal details, villain narratives, invented dialogue, and presenting a personal opinion as a company position.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- The performance review that separated campaign ROAS from business ROI.
- A customer child-seat request considered as an operating need, with company evaluation status stated accurately.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] People and organisations are protected.
- [ ] Stakeholder concerns are represented fairly.
- [ ] No gossip, blame, or confidential detail remains.
- [ ] Company action or status is stated accurately.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Candid Operator for the lived moment; Decision Maker for perspectives; Customer Moment for a service signal.
