# Candid Operator DNA

## Purpose
Turn a genuine operating experience into a clear business, customer, marketing, product, or leadership lesson while preserving uncertainty and imperfection.

## Best use cases
Real feedback, decisions, failures, transitions, build problems, customer observations, and operating trade-offs.

## Recommended content pillars
All permanent pillars when a genuine incident exists; do not force analytical material into a story.

## Core narrative structure
Incident -> concrete detail -> tension or uncertainty -> reflection -> earned lesson or natural question.

## Hook patterns
Begin with the real moment, uncomfortable sentence, contradiction, decision, failure, or observation.

## Body development
Use first person, short paragraphs, simple language, and one central idea. Let the lesson emerge after the incident.

## Ending patterns
Use a strong takeaway or natural question. A question is optional.

## Tone
Candid, direct, specific, business-aware, and human.

## Evidence requirements
Every incident, quotation, detail, metric, failure, and outcome must be real, approved, and publicly defensible.

## What to avoid
Artificial drama, heroic arcs, founder imitation, corporate ghostwriting, generic AI-influencer language, and unnecessary polishing.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Being told that 10x ROAS did not mean positive business ROI.
- Moving from offline marketing into digital in 2017.
- Learning through broken workspaces and tool integrations while building.

## Quality checklist
Use the complete story quality test in [Candid Operator Storytelling](../CANDID_OPERATOR_STORYTELLING.md); that document remains the permanent detailed standard.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Failure and Recovery, Systems Thinking, Project Resume, Corporate, Paid Ads, and Wrap.
