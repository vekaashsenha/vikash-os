# Buyer Psychology DNA

## Purpose
Explain the rational, emotional, political, and risk-related forces behind a buyer's action, delay, objection, or decision.

## Best use cases
Status quo, willingness to pay, trust, objections, career risk, evaluation criteria, adoption friction, and no-decision outcomes.

## Recommended content pillars
Buyer Psychology With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, Mobility With Vikash, and AI Visibility Journal.

## Core narrative structure
Buying context -> observed behaviour -> possible driver or fear -> evidence needed -> marketing, sales, or product implication.

## Hook patterns
- "The biggest competitor in B2B is often no decision."
- Open with a defensible buyer behaviour, not mind-reading.

## Body development
Separate observed behaviour from interpretation. Consider trust, perceived risk, effort, internal alignment, and value without assuming a single motive.

## Ending patterns
End with what the buyer needs to believe, de-risk, or decide.

## Tone
Curious, evidence-led, commercially aware, and non-judgmental.

## Evidence requirements
Use reviewed VIIF research, approved direct evidence, or clearly labelled observation and hypothesis.

## What to avoid
Mind-reading, stereotypes, universal buyer claims, invented objections, and treating one example as a segment truth.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Repeated free-trial accounts as a question about willingness to pay, not proof of motive.
- Why no decision can be a more relevant B2B competitor than another supplier.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Observation and interpretation are distinct.
- [ ] Buyer motives are not invented.
- [ ] Risk and trade-offs are visible.
- [ ] Research or evidence status is clear.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Decision Maker for stakeholder roles; SaaS Success for product behaviour; Contrarian Truth for a challenged belief.
