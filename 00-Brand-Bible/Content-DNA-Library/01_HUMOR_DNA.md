# Humor DNA

## Purpose
Use light, intelligent humour to make a real professional observation easier to recognise and remember. The insight must remain stronger than the joke.

## Best use cases
- Mildly absurd build loops, workflow friction, or familiar work contradictions.
- A supporting layer for a serious lesson that does not need solemn delivery.

## Recommended content pillars
Build With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, Leadership With Vikash, and Mobility With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Real observation -> recognisable contradiction -> restrained humorous line -> practical insight.

## Hook patterns
- "I fixed one problem. The system responded by introducing me to the next one."
- A direct observation whose truth creates the humour without a punchline.

## Body development
Describe only the real situation. Let timing, contrast, or understatement carry the humour, then move quickly to what the incident revealed.

## Ending patterns
End with the business lesson or a specific question. Do not chase a final joke.

## Tone
Warm, observant, self-aware, and never cruel.

## Evidence requirements
The event and all details must be genuine. Humour does not permit invented dialogue, reactions, or exaggeration.

## What to avoid
Ridicule, forced jokes, memes, sarcasm aimed at a person, confidential embarrassment, and humour that weakens the insight.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Fixing a broken workspace only to uncover the next system dependency.
- Learning that product building involved repeated testing, breaking, and repair.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The observation happened.
- [ ] No person or group is the target.
- [ ] The post still works if the humorous line is removed.
- [ ] The insight is stronger than the joke.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Candid Operator for the real incident; Systems Thinking for the underlying dependency; Wrap for a restrained close.
