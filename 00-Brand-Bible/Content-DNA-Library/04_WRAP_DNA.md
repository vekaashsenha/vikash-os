# Wrap DNA

## Purpose
Provide a clean, earned ending to a post led by another DNA. Wrap is supporting DNA, not a complete content format.

## Best use cases
When the story or analysis is complete but needs a concise takeaway, unresolved tension, practical action, or thoughtful question.

## Recommended content pillars
All permanent pillars; Wrap should never determine the pillar.

## Core narrative structure
Primary DNA completes its argument -> one closing move that follows directly from it.

## Hook patterns
Not applicable. Wrap does not create the opening.

## Body development
Not applicable. Use the selected primary DNA for the body.

## Ending patterns
- Earned takeaway.
- Specific unresolved tension.
- Practical next question.
- Honest current-state statement.
- Natural discussion question for experienced readers.

## Tone
Concise, calm, specific, and proportionate to the evidence.

## Evidence requirements
The ending must not introduce a new claim, outcome, certainty, or call to action unsupported by the body.

## What to avoid
Generic questions, promotional endings, motivational slogans, recap paragraphs, and a sudden heroic conclusion.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Closing the ROAS-versus-business-ROI story with the question of profitable growth.
- Closing a build failure with the exact system improvement made, not a victory claim.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The ending follows from the post.
- [ ] It adds no unsupported claim.
- [ ] It is not generic or promotional.
- [ ] Removing it would leave the post less complete, not merely shorter.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Any primary DNA, especially Candid Operator, Customer Moment, Step Guide, and Contrarian Truth.
