# Business Diagnosis DNA

## Purpose
Separate a visible business symptom from the underlying condition, constraints, and evidence needed before choosing a response.

## Best use cases
Negative ROI despite strong campaign metrics, weak conversion, operational bottlenecks, demand problems, retention questions, and AGMP diagnosis lessons.

## Recommended content pillars
AGMP With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, Buyer Psychology With Vikash, and Mobility With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Visible symptom -> possible underlying conditions -> evidence and process review -> diagnosis boundary -> appropriate business response.

## Hook patterns
- "The visible problem was not necessarily the real problem."
- Start with the symptom-and-cause mismatch.

## Body development
Test multiple explanations across pricing, margin, conversion, operations, retention, capacity, or customer flow only where relevant. Use diagnosis as an explanatory business analogy, not medical authority.

## Ending patterns
End with the question that must be answered before prescribing a business solution.

## Tone
Analytical, careful, practical, and non-dramatic.

## Evidence requirements
Symptoms must be verified; causes must be supported or labelled as hypotheses. The framework is explanatory and is not medical advice.

## What to avoid
Medical advice, false certainty, single-cause claims without evidence, diagnosing people, and prescribing tactics before understanding the system.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- The AGMP lesson about diagnosing the real business problem rather than treating symptoms.
- Why positive ROAS could coexist with negative business ROI.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Symptom and cause are separated.
- [ ] Multiple plausible conditions were considered.
- [ ] Evidence status is explicit.
- [ ] The analogy remains business explanation, not medical advice.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Systems Thinking for dependencies; Paid Ads for commercial measures; Marketing Myth for an oversimplified belief.
