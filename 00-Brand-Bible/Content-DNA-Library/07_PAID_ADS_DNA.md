# Paid Ads DNA

## Purpose
Connect paid-media activity to verified commercial outcomes instead of treating platform efficiency as business success.

## Best use cases
ROAS, CPL, lead quality, conversion, margin, attribution, channel economics, and campaign-review lessons.

## Recommended content pillars
Marketing Beyond Marketing and Buyer Psychology With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Campaign signal -> wider commercial context -> missing measure or constraint -> business implication -> decision.

## Hook patterns
- "My ROAS was 10x. The business ROI was still negative."
- Start with the verified contrast between the platform result and the business result.

## Body development
Explain platform metrics in plain language, then connect them to pricing, margins, sales conversion, retention, operations, and cash flow only where the facts support it.

## Ending patterns
End with the commercial question that should be asked before celebrating performance.

## Tone
Business-first, candid, numerate, and non-technical where possible.

## Evidence requirements
Verify every number, definition, time period, and outcome. Do not claim a campaign harmed the business unless that fact is established.

## What to avoid
Vanity-metric celebration, unverified attribution, benchmark theatre, platform jargon, and implying causation from correlation.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- The review where ROAS reached 10x while business ROI was described as negative.
- Why marketing metrics alone were insufficient for a business-ROI discussion.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] All numbers and business claims are verified.
- [ ] Platform and business measures are distinguished.
- [ ] No unsupported harm or causation claim remains.
- [ ] The commercial decision is clear.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Business Diagnosis for the wider condition; Marketing Myth for a belief correction; Candid Operator for the review moment.
