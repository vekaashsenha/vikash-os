# AI Builder DNA

## Purpose
Document genuine AI-assisted product and workflow building from the perspective of an experienced business professional who is still learning the technical craft.

## Best use cases
Build attempts, prompt improvements, repository work, integrations, testing, limitations, QueryCite decisions, and automation lessons.

## Recommended content pillars
Build With Vikash and AI Visibility Journal.

## Core narrative structure
Real problem -> attempt -> friction or result -> what Vikash learned -> system or next test.

## Hook patterns
- "Then I started building. The idea was the easy part."
- Open with the attempt, error, trade-off, or unexpected dependency.

## Body development
State the tool's role and Vikash's role accurately. Describe what was tried, tested, broken, fixed, or remains uncertain without presenting AI assistance as independent expertise.

## Ending patterns
End with the current learning, next test, or system improvement.

## Tone
Curious, specific, candid, practical, and free of AI hype.

## Evidence requirements
Use real build logs, repository evidence, test outputs, or verified product state. Distinguish prototype, MVP, test, launch, and scale.

## What to avoid
Positioning Vikash as a senior engineer, invented productivity gains, "AI built everything" claims, generic tool worship, and exaggerated product maturity.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Understanding Git, GitHub, repositories, permissions, workspaces, tools, prompts, and workflows while building.
- Why the QueryCite problem led Vikash to build and learn in public.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] The build activity is genuine.
- [ ] Human and AI contributions are represented accurately.
- [ ] Technical capability and product maturity are not inflated.
- [ ] The lesson is specific enough to be useful.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Project Resume for credible proof; Failure and Recovery for an error path; Systems Thinking for dependencies.
