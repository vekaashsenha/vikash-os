# Decision Maker DNA

## Purpose
Examine a business decision through the different responsibilities, risks, evidence needs, and trade-offs of the people involved.

## Best use cases
Buying committees, leadership reviews, cross-functional decisions, investment choices, and operating-policy trade-offs.

## Recommended content pillars
Buyer Psychology With Vikash, Marketing Beyond Marketing, Leadership With Vikash, and Mobility With Vikash.

## Core narrative structure
Decision -> relevant perspectives -> distinct outcomes or risks -> trade-off -> implication for communication or action.

## Hook patterns
- "The same proposal can mean growth to one stakeholder and risk to another."
- Start with the decision tension, not a generic persona list.

## Body development
Explain only roles and concerns supported by experience or reviewed research. Show how a reasonable decision can differ by accountability and evidence needs.

## Ending patterns
End with the perspective missing from the current decision or the evidence needed to align it.

## Tone
Balanced, precise, empathetic, and commercially grounded.

## Evidence requirements
Use verified stakeholder roles, reviewed VIIF research, or clearly labelled hypotheses. Preserve confidentiality.

## What to avoid
Invented stakeholder motives, caricatures, simplistic authority maps, and implying that one function is irrational.

## Example topics based on genuine experience
- Why marketing metrics did not satisfy a business-ROI review.
- Different outcomes sought by stakeholders in a B2B buying committee.

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Each perspective is legitimate and supported.
- [ ] Roles are not confused with motives.
- [ ] The trade-off is explicit.
- [ ] Confidentiality is protected.

## Strong combinations with other DNA
Buyer Psychology for risk and belief; Corporate for safe context; Systems Thinking for downstream effects.
