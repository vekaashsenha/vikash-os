# Industry Intelligence Research SOP

Use this SOP when creating an industry research asset through the Vikash Industry Intelligence Framework.

The goal is to create practical, sourced, reusable industry intelligence. Do not publish research, LinkedIn posts, or playbooks until assumptions and gaps are reviewed.

## Operating Process

1. Select and narrowly define the industry.
2. Approve the research question.
3. Gather verified primary and secondary sources.
4. Complete industry landscape.
5. Analyze business model.
6. Segment the market.
7. Define ICP.
8. Map buying committee.
9. Build personas.
10. Map buying journey.
11. Analyze buying psychology.
12. Build decision framework.
13. Translate into marketing strategy.
14. Translate into sales strategy.
15. Identify customer success and AI opportunities.
16. Add Vikash's practical perspective.
17. Create final knowledge and content assets.
18. Review assumptions and gaps.
19. Approve before publication.

## Research Quality Checklist

- Is the industry narrowly defined?
- Are adjacent categories separated?
- Are facts sourced?
- Are assumptions labelled?
- Is the ICP an organization, not a person?
- Are personas based on the buying committee?
- Are decision power and influence separated?
- Are marketing and sales recommendations linked to research?
- Are AI opportunities realistic?
- Has personal experience not been invented?
- Does the final writing sound human?

## Source Discipline

Use a mix of sources where available:

- Company websites and annual reports.
- Industry reports.
- Government or regulatory sources.
- Analyst commentary.
- Customer reviews and public feedback.
- Job descriptions and role expectations.
- Sales notes or field observations where permitted.
- Interviews or conversations where consent and confidentiality allow.

Do not treat unsupported claims as facts.

## Output Review

Before finalizing an industry study, confirm:

- The audience for the research is clear.
- The decision supported by the research is clear.
- Every recommendation can be traced back to research or labelled experience.
- Anything uncertain is marked as an assumption or gap.
- The public content angle does not overstate the research.
