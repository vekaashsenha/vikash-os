# Monthly Review

Run this once per month. Time limit: **30–45 minutes**.

## 1. Prepare — 5 minutes

Open:

- [Control Centre](../VIKASHOS_CONTROL_CENTRE.md)
- [Story Bank](08_STORY_BANK.md)
- [Content Calendar](09_CONTENT_CALENDAR.md)
- [Performance Dashboard](10_PERFORMANCE_DASHBOARD.md)

Confirm that published URLs and available metrics have been recorded. Do not fill gaps with estimates.

## 2. Review published work — 10 minutes

- Which posts performed best, and on which measures?
- Which posts were weak or unclear?
- Which hooks earned thoughtful attention?
- Which posts generated substantive comments, saves, or conversations?
- Which formats worked best?

## 3. Review themes and audience — 10 minutes

- What were the strongest themes?
- What did the audience repeatedly respond to?
- Which ideas were repeated too often?
- Which important content gaps remain?
- Did the posts strengthen the intended professional positioning?

## 4. Review the Story Bank and series — 5–10 minutes

For each series ask:

- Is there a healthy supply of real, safe-to-share ideas?
- Should the series continue at its current frequency?
- Does it overlap with another series?
- Is more evidence or research required?

## 5. Review the workflow — 5 minutes

- Where did Vikash spend the most time?
- Which approval step created delay?
- Which tool failed or created rework?
- Did any step silently depend on manual memory?
- What one bottleneck should be fixed next month?

## 6. Review the posting-time experiment — 5–10 minutes

Use [the Posting-Time Experiment](15_POSTING_TIME_EXPERIMENT.md) and assess:

- Results by posting time.
- Results by weekday.
- Results by permanent series.
- Results by format and media type.
- Confirmed sample size for every window.
- Median reach metrics and median quality metrics.
- Whether timing differences are meaningful or could be explained by topic, format, series, holiday, major event, or unusual conditions.
- Whether each window has at least three posts for a preliminary signal.
- Whether six or more posts are available before recommending a winner.
- Whether further controlled testing is needed.

Do not declare a best time from one post or change the default schedule from a weekly fluctuation.

## 7. Approve next-month changes — 5 minutes

Permitted changes:

- Simplify one template or step.
- Clarify one ownership boundary.
- Change a series frequency based on evidence.
- Add an automation only after a repeated bottleneck is proven.
- Archive an obsolete file only after a separate approval.
- Change the default posting time only when the experiment has sufficient evidence and Vikash approves the monthly recommendation.

## Stability Rule

**Do not redesign VikashOS every week.** Use the system consistently for a month. Change it only when a real repeated bottleneck, error, or missing decision point is documented.
