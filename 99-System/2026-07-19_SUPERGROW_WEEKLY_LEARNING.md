## 5. What Appears to Be Working

- Personal-transition storytelling currently appears strongest for engagement depth and audience connection.
- The focused AGMP lesson currently appears strongest for reach and authority.
- Personal stories and AGMP insights appear to perform different content jobs: connection and trust versus authority and distribution.
- The product-building post had low reach but a reasonable calculated engagement rate among the people reached.
- Specific, experience-led material remains more useful for learning than generic content categories alone.

These observations apply only to this one-week sample.

## 6. What Appears Weak

- The ROAS post had the lowest reach-adjusted response and the fewest total engagements.
- A plausible editorial explanation is that the central conflict appeared too late, the opening took too long to reach the tension, and the programme mention at the end may have weakened the personal story's authenticity.
- That explanation is a hypothesis, not proven causation.
- Conversation was absent in the reported results: zero comments and zero reposts across the four posts.
- Generic end questions should not be forced as a remedy. Where discussion is useful, a specific unresolved business tension may give experienced readers something concrete to answer.

## 7. Data-Quality Limitations

- The report covers only one week and four Supergrow-published posts.
- `27 ÷ 4` equals `6.75`, not the reported average of `6`. Preserve both the raw total and reported average until Supergrow's rounding or calculation method is known.
- The reported `+135%` versus a stated 30-day average of `2 engagements per post` does not reconcile through a simple comparison with either `6` or `6.75`. Supergrow may use an internal baseline or method that was not supplied.
- Exact report-period boundaries and measurement windows were not supplied.
- Likes and total engagements are equal for each post in the source figures, while comments and reposts are reported as zero. Other engagement components were not supplied.
- No reliable post-level attribution was supplied for profile views, follower gains, direct messages, or relevant conversations.
- Topic, series, weekday, and ending style changed across posts, so this is not a controlled test of any one variable.
- The reported best-performing day is a source observation, not enough evidence for a posting-day decision.

## 8. Provisional Hypotheses

The following require more evidence and must not be treated as permanent rules:

- Tuesday is the best posting day.
- Friday should be avoided.
- Self-reflective questions are always the best hook.
- Personal stories always outperform analytical posts.
- AGMP posts always produce greater reach.
- Programme mentions always reduce performance.
- Product-building posts receive weak distribution.

Current narrower hypotheses:

- A genuine contradiction or difficult statement placed earlier may improve attention without exaggeration.
- Personal-transition stories may deepen audience connection when they contain specific, credible details.
- Focused AGMP lessons may support reach and authority.
- A promotional ending may weaken an otherwise personal story when the promotion is not essential to the lesson.
- A specific unresolved tension may be more likely to generate useful discussion than a generic engagement question.

## 9. Decisions for Future Drafting

Apply these as provisional drafting tests to future unscheduled posts:

- Begin with the real moment, contradiction, difficult question, or uncomfortable statement sooner.
- Preserve specific details.
- Let the lesson follow the incident.
- Do not add a programme or product promotion to the end of an otherwise personal story.
- Do not exaggerate consequences to create a stronger hook.
- Continue using personal-transition stories selectively.
- Continue publishing focused AGMP lessons.
- Keep one central idea per post.
- When seeking discussion, use a specific tension rather than a generic engagement question.

Do not use Supergrow's suggested hook, “My 10x ROAS campaign was killing the business.” The known facts do not establish that claim. A credible alternative is to put the genuine feedback or contradiction earlier without inventing a business consequence.

## 10. What Will Not Change Yet

- The current 08:30 AM IST experiment in `Asia/Kolkata` continues as scheduled.
- No currently scheduled or published post will be modified, rescheduled, deleted, or resubmitted.
- No posting-day strategy changes after one week of data.
- Friday is not classified as a poor day from one low-reach post.
- Tuesday is not declared the best day.
- Existing series frequencies and permanent writing rules remain unchanged.
- Questions will not become mandatory at the end of every post.

## 11. Evidence Required Before a Permanent Rule

Collect at least four comparable weeks before considering a posting-day or broad series-performance rule. For each future post, record:

- Series.
- Format.
- Hook type.
- Story-led or analytical.
- Impressions.
- Reactions.
- Comments.
- Reposts.
- Total engagements.
- Engagement rate.
- Profile views, when available.
- Followers gained, when attributable.
- Direct messages or relevant conversations.
- Publication day.
- Publication time in `Asia/Kolkata`.
- Promotional ending: yes or no.
- Specific discussion tension: yes or no.

### Four-week review checkpoint

After four comparable weekly periods are available, compare:

- Personal-transition stories.
- AGMP insights.
- Marketing operator stories.
- Build With Vikash posts.
- Other content pillars.
- Weekday performance.
- Reach versus engagement depth.
- Comment-generation ability.

Use medians and sample sizes where possible. Separate reach from engagement depth, note confounding variables, and make a permanent change only through the approved monthly review.
