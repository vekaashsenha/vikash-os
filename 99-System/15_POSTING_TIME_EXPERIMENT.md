## Experiment Rules

1. Change posting time first; avoid changing several major variables simultaneously.
2. Record series, topic, format, length, weekday, time, media type, and post-publication engagement activity.
3. Measure at 2 hours, 24 hours, 72 hours, and 7 days.
4. Separate reach metrics from quality metrics.
5. Require at least three posts per time window for a preliminary signal.
6. Prefer six or more posts per time window before recommending a winner.
7. Use median performance, not only averages.
8. Never declare a universal best time from one post.
9. Record holidays, major events, platform issues, paid amplification, or unusual conditions.
10. Do not fabricate unavailable data; use `Pending`.
11. Never change an approved scheduled date or time without explicit approval.
12. Do not repeatedly use the same test window while another approved window remains unused, unless the phase requires replication.
13. Collect at least four comparable weeks before proposing a posting-day strategy change.
14. Record hook type, story-led or analytical approach, promotional ending, and presence of a specific discussion tension alongside timing data.

## Metrics

### Reach metrics

- Impressions
- Viewers or members reached
- Reactions

### Quality metrics

- Comments
- Saves
- Reposts
- Profile views
- Followers gained
- Relevant direct messages
- Meaningful professional conversations

`Quality engagements` should be reported as the available total of comments, saves, reposts, relevant direct messages, and meaningful professional conversations. Keep component values visible; do not hide them behind one score.

## Experiment Phases

### Phase 1 — Baseline

Evaluate confirmed results from existing 09:00 AM IST posts. Do not count scheduled records as published samples until verified.

### Phase 2 — Morning comparison

Compare 08:30 AM with 10:30 AM using reasonably comparable posts. Rotate windows instead of filling one window first when practical.

### Phase 3 — Daypart comparison

Compare the strongest morning window with 01:00 PM and 06:30 PM.

### Phase 4 — Validation

Retest the two strongest windows with comparable posts across relevant series and formats.

### Phase 5 — Recommendation

Recommend:

- Primary posting time
- Secondary posting time
- Time by series, only where justified
- Windows to avoid, only where justified
- Sample size
- Confidence level
- Remaining uncertainty

## Four-Week Review Checkpoint

After four comparable weekly periods, compare:

- Weekday performance across reasonably comparable posts.
- Personal-transition, AGMP, marketing operator, Build With Vikash, and other pillar results.
- Reach versus engagement depth.
- Comments and reposts as conversation and redistribution signals.
- Hook type, story-led versus analytical approach, promotional ending, and specific discussion tension.

Do not identify a winning or avoided weekday unless the evidence survives these comparisons and the monthly review approves a change.

## Confidence Scale

| Confidence | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Insufficient | Fewer than three comparable confirmed posts per relevant window |
| Preliminary | At least three comparable posts per window; more testing required |
| Moderate | Six or more posts per window with a consistent median pattern |
| Strong | Repeated pattern across validation, series, formats, and normal conditions |
