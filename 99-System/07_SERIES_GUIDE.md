# Series Guide

VikashOS v1.1 has **seven permanent public series**. Additional names in this guide are editorial categories, recurring formats, sub-series, or aliases within those seven series. They do not create independent permanent series or new folders.

## Seven Permanent Series

| Permanent series | Canonical repository home | Related category, format, or alias |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Marketing Beyond Marketing | `01-LinkedIn/Marketing-Beyond-Marketing/` | Marketing Beyond Marketing |
| AGMP With Vikash | `01-LinkedIn/AGMP/` | AGMP With Vikash |
| Build With Vikash | `01-LinkedIn/Build-With-Vikash/` | Build With Vikash |
| Mobility With Vikash | `01-LinkedIn/Mobility/` | Mobility and Travel Insights |
| Buyer Psychology With Vikash | `01-LinkedIn/Buyer-Psychology/` | Inside the Buying Committee |
| Leadership With Vikash | `01-LinkedIn/Leadership/` | Leadership Journal |
| AI Visibility Journal | `01-LinkedIn/AI-Visibility-Journal/` | AI visibility, AI search, GEO, AEO, customer discovery, and QueryCite build lessons |

`Build-With-Vikash` is the canonical permanent folder. `Build-With-AI` is a legacy or overlapping folder that must remain unchanged until a later content-by-content review.

## Editorial Categories and Aliases

| Category, format, or alias | Parent permanent series | Operational rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Leadership Journal | Leadership With Vikash | Use for first-person leadership and career reflections. Store in `Leadership`. |
| Inside the Buying Committee | Buyer Psychology With Vikash | Use for research-led stakeholder and decision-group analysis. Store in `Buyer-Psychology`. |
| Mobility and Travel Insights | Mobility With Vikash | Use for travel, mobility, service, and operational observations. Store in `Mobility`. |
| AI Tools and Automation | Build With Vikash | Use for practical tools, prompts, and automation. Store public posts in `Build-With-Vikash`; supporting notes may live in the AI Knowledge Vault. |


Do not create separate series folders for these categories or aliases.
## Writing-Style Application

[Candid Operator Storytelling](../00-Brand-Bible/CANDID_OPERATOR_STORYTELLING.md) is the preferred style when a post in the following lanes is supported by a genuine incident:

| Series or lane | Application |
| --- | --- |
| Marketing Beyond Marketing | Preferred for real marketing, commercial, reporting, campaign, lead-quality, or alignment incidents. |
| Inside the Buying Committee | Preferred when a real buyer, stakeholder, or decision-group observation is available; do not invent buyer behaviour. |
| Leadership Journal | Preferred for genuine feedback, disagreement, decision, failure, or team situations. |
| Build With Vikash | Preferred for real build attempts, errors, trade-offs, experiments, and product decisions. |
| Mobility and Travel Insights | Preferred for genuine service, travel, mobility, or customer-friction observations. |
| AI Visibility Journal | Preferred for real founder, marketer, customer, or product-building problems; QueryCite remains secondary to the insight. |
| AGMP With Vikash | Use only when there is a genuine classroom, peer, or workplace incident. Do not force case analysis into storytelling. |

This preference applies to story-led posts, not every post. Analytical, educational, practical, framework-based, and progress formats remain valid. End with a strong takeaway or a natural question; do not force a question.

Recommended monthly mix:

- 50% candid operator stories.
- 20% analytical or educational posts.
- 15% practical frameworks or observations.
- 15% product-building, experiments, or progress updates.
## Leadership With Vikash — Leadership Journal Format

- **Purpose:** Share honest career, feedback, decision, and leadership lessons.
- **Audience:** Managers, mid-career professionals, and emerging leaders.
- **Tone:** Personal, calm, useful; never manufactured vulnerability.
- **Suitable topics:** Feedback moments, career transitions, difficult decisions, communication, learning.
- **Avoid:** Generic inspiration, confidential people details, performative failure stories.
- **Frequency:** Up to twice per month or when a strong real story exists.
- **Typical structure:** Moment → reflection → leadership lesson → question.
- **CTA style:** Ask for a comparable professional lesson.
- **Example:** [MANIFESTO-001](../01-LinkedIn/Leadership/MANIFESTO-001.md).

## Marketing Beyond Marketing

- **Purpose:** Connect marketing activity to business economics, strategy, operations, and growth.
- **Audience:** CEOs, founders, CMOs, growth leaders, and marketers.
- **Tone:** Business-first, practical, evidence-backed.
- **Suitable topics:** ROI, margins, demand, revenue alignment, operational consequences, brand trust.
- **Avoid:** Platform tricks, unsupported benchmarks, vanity-metric commentary.
- **Frequency:** Two to four times per month.
- **Typical structure:** Marketing observation → business consequence → one lesson → question.
- **CTA style:** Invite readers to examine the wider business system.
- **Example folder:** [MBM-001](../01-LinkedIn/Marketing-Beyond-Marketing/MBM-001/).

## AGMP With Vikash

- **Purpose:** Turn management learning into practical business reflection.
- **Audience:** Business leaders, AGMP or MBA learners, and functional managers.
- **Tone:** Reflective and applied, not academic or authoritative.
- **Suitable topics:** Strategy, finance, operations, economics, leadership, decision-making.
- **Avoid:** Private classroom material, professor quotations without permission, case facts not cleared for use.
- **Frequency:** Two to four times per month when there is a real applied lesson.
- **Typical structure:** Learning → changed perspective → business application → question.
- **CTA style:** Ask how the concept appears in the reader's business.
- **Example folder:** [AGMP-001](../01-LinkedIn/AGMP/AGMP-001/).

## Build With Vikash

- **Purpose:** Document practical AI and product-building work, including failures and system lessons.
- **Audience:** AI learners, product builders, operators, and mid-career professionals learning to build.
- **Tone:** Honest build log; curious, specific, and free of hype.
- **Suitable topics:** Git, repositories, Codex, workflows, QueryCite, testing, automation, small products.
- **Avoid:** Fake launches, inflated achievements, unsupported claims about AI.
- **Frequency:** Two to four times per month while active building continues.
- **Typical structure:** Attempt → friction → lesson → better system → question.
- **CTA style:** Ask about the reader's build experience or unexpected constraint.
- **Example folder:** [BWV-001](../01-LinkedIn/Build-With-Vikash/BWV-001/).


## AI Visibility Journal

- **Purpose:** Help founders, marketers, and business leaders understand how AI is changing search, discovery, trust, and brand visibility.
- **Audience:** Founders, small-business owners, B2B marketers, marketing leaders, SaaS teams, SEO professionals, agencies, and businesses assessing AI discoverability.
- **Tone:** Educational, candid, evidence-aware, and restrained; teach before promoting.
- **Suitable topics:** AI visibility, AI search behaviour, GEO, AEO, SEO evolution, customer discovery, visibility in AI answers, QueryCite build experiments, and approved user lessons.
- **Avoid:** Product-first openings, invented conversations or audit results, unsupported claims that AI replaced Google or SEO is dead, confidential customer information, criticism of founders or agencies, and exaggerated QueryCite maturity or outcomes.
- **Frequency:** Approximately once every two weeks at first, rotated into the current six-post volume when the strongest genuine story supports it.
- **Scheduling rule:** Replace the least relevant or weakest pillar for that week without permanently removing it. Do not add a seventh weekly post or mandatory Sunday slot.
- **Typical story structure:** Real problem or incident → changing discovery question → business or marketing insight → natural QueryCite connection when relevant → earned takeaway.
- **Other valid formats:** Analytical, educational, and practical posts when no genuine incident exists.
- **Evidence rule:** Label observations, hypotheses, experiments, and confirmed evidence clearly.
- **Performance rule:** Do not attribute QueryCite visits or sign-ups to LinkedIn without reliable tracking.
- **Canonical home:** [AI Visibility Journal](../01-LinkedIn/AI-Visibility-Journal/).
## Buyer Psychology With Vikash — Inside the Buying Committee Format

- **Purpose:** Explain how stakeholder roles shape B2B buying decisions.
- **Audience:** CEOs, marketing and sales leaders, product leaders, and account teams.
- **Tone:** Research-led, precise, and careful about assumptions.
- **Suitable topics:** Buying committees, influence, decision power, proof needs, internal risk.
- **Avoid:** Invented buyer behaviour, broad industry generalizations, unsourced claims.
- **Frequency:** One to two times per month after VIIF research is reviewed.
- **Typical structure:** Industry boundary → stakeholders → different outcomes sought → implications.
- **CTA style:** Ask which stakeholder changes the decision most.
- **Example:** [BP-001 structured draft](../01-LinkedIn/Buyer-Psychology/BP-001.md).

## Mobility With Vikash — Mobility and Travel Insights Category

- **Purpose:** Explore mobility, travel, service operations, and customer experience.
- **Audience:** Travel leaders, operators, marketers, procurement, HR, and business leaders.
- **Tone:** Observational, operational, and grounded.
- **Suitable topics:** Reliability, service recovery, policy, employee experience, travel technology, operational friction.
- **Avoid:** Unsupported industry statistics, client details, generic claims about travellers.
- **Frequency:** One to two times per month when supported by experience or research.
- **Typical structure:** Service observation → operational cause → business implication → question.
- **CTA style:** Ask about a real service or operational trade-off.
- **Existing home:** [Mobility series guide](../01-LinkedIn/Mobility/README.md).

## Build With Vikash — AI Tools and Automation Category

- **Purpose:** Share practical use of AI tools and automation in real workflows.
- **Audience:** Business professionals, operators, marketers, and AI learners.
- **Tone:** Practical and cautious; human judgment remains visible.
- **Suitable topics:** Tool selection, workflow design, failure recovery, prompt lessons, automation limits.
- **Avoid:** AI hype, replacement claims, invented productivity gains, generic tool lists.
- **Frequency:** As part of Build With Vikash, up to twice per month.
- **Typical structure:** Task → tool or workflow → limitation → useful lesson.
- **CTA style:** Ask where human judgment or workflow design mattered.
- **Existing homes:** [AI Knowledge Vault](../02-Knowledge-Vault/AI/) and [Build With Vikash](../01-LinkedIn/Build-With-Vikash/).

## Buyer Psychology With Vikash — Broader Buyer Psychology Category

- **Purpose:** Examine rational, emotional, political, and risk-related drivers in B2B decisions.
- **Audience:** Marketing, sales, product, and customer-success leaders.
- **Tone:** Curious and evidence-led; distinguish facts, observations, and hypotheses.
- **Suitable topics:** Triggers, fears, trust, objections, status quo, career risk, evaluation criteria.
- **Avoid:** Mind-reading, stereotypes, or unsupported claims about industries or roles.
- **Frequency:** One to two times per month after VIIF review.
- **Typical structure:** Buying context → driver or fear → evidence needed → marketing implication.
- **CTA style:** Ask what buyers need to believe or de-risk.
- **Example:** [BP-001 structured draft](../01-LinkedIn/Buyer-Psychology/BP-001.md).
