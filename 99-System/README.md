# VikashOS v1.1 Operating System

`99-System` is the weekly control layer for VikashOS. It connects ideas, drafting, approval, Codex production, GitHub, Supergrow, optional Canva, scheduling, recovery, and performance review.

It does not replace the Brand Bible, Knowledge Vault, templates, SOPs, or project folders. It tells Vikash how to use them together with 2–3 hours of weekly attention.

## Dashboard Roles

- [VikashOS Control Centre](../VIKASHOS_CONTROL_CENTRE.md): first file for weekly operations, approvals, bottlenecks, and next actions.
- [Content Performance Dashboard](10_PERFORMANCE_DASHBOARD.md): post-level metrics, timing analysis, and content-performance lessons.
- [Root Strategic Dashboard](../DASHBOARD.md): broader strategy, projects, six operating systems, and long-term direction.

These files have distinct purposes and should not be merged or treated as duplicates.
## Permanent Writing Standard

[Candid Operator Storytelling](../00-Brand-Bible/CANDID_OPERATOR_STORYTELLING.md) is the primary style for story-led LinkedIn posts supported by genuine incidents.

It does not replace analytical, educational, practical, framework-based, or progress posts. The recommended monthly mix is 50% candid operator stories, 20% analytical or educational posts, 15% practical frameworks or observations, and 15% product-building, experiments, or progress updates.

Use the [Content-Creation Prompt](03_CONTENT_CREATION_PROMPT.md) to draft and the [Content Review Checklist](04_CONTENT_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md) to run the story quality test. Never force a story, question, product mention, emotional arc, or heroic conclusion.
## Open This First

Start every week with the [VikashOS Control Centre](../VIKASHOS_CONTROL_CENTRE.md).

Then use the [Weekly Workflow](01_WEEKLY_WORKFLOW.md) to move only the strongest ideas through the system.

## Weekly Use

1. Open the [Control Centre](../VIKASHOS_CONTROL_CENTRE.md).
2. Capture three to five ideas with the [Idea Capture Template](02_IDEA_CAPTURE_TEMPLATE.md).
3. Add or update them in the [Story Bank](08_STORY_BANK.md).
4. Select the strongest one to three ideas.
5. Give ChatGPT the selected entries and [Content-Creation Prompt](03_CONTENT_CREATION_PROMPT.md).
6. For a genuine story-led idea, apply [Candid Operator Storytelling](../00-Brand-Bible/CANDID_OPERATOR_STORYTELLING.md).
7. Review drafts with the [Content Review Checklist](04_CONTENT_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md).
8. After production approval, give Codex the [Production Prompt](05_CODEX_PRODUCTION_PROMPT.md).
9. Review the five-file package.
10. After publication approval, give Codex the [Publishing and Scheduling Prompt](06_PUBLISHING_AND_SCHEDULING_PROMPT.md).
11. Update the [Calendar](09_CONTENT_CALENDAR.md), [Performance Dashboard](10_PERFORMANCE_DASHBOARD.md), and Control Centre.

## What to Give ChatGPT

- The selected Story Bank entry.
- Known facts and evidence.
- Details that are private or prohibited.
- The intended series from the [Series Guide](07_SERIES_GUIDE.md).
- The reusable [Content-Creation Prompt](03_CONTENT_CREATION_PROMPT.md).

ChatGPT creates a draft. It does not approve, publish, or fill factual gaps.

## What to Give Codex

Before approval:

- Exact approved post wording.
- Target series and post ID.
- [Codex Production Prompt](05_CODEX_PRODUCTION_PROMPT.md).

After publication approval:

- Approved post folder.
- Exact content and metadata commit messages.
- Exact schedule with timezone.
- Supergrow score threshold.
- [Publishing and Scheduling Prompt](06_PUBLISHING_AND_SCHEDULING_PROMPT.md).

## When Vikash Approves

Vikash approves at three points:

1. **Topic selection:** Is this idea worth developing?
2. **Production approval:** Is the wording true, safe, useful, and in Vikash's voice?
3. **Publication approval:** Are the final files, external actions, and exact schedule approved?

Use the statuses in [the review checklist](04_CONTENT_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md).

## When to Publish

Publish only when:

- The wording is approved for publication.
- Confidentiality and evidence checks are complete.
- GitHub contains the approved content commit.
- The schedule has an explicit day, date, time, year, and timezone.
- Supergrow state is confirmed.

Canva is optional.

## When Something Fails

Open the [Failure Recovery Guide](13_FAILURE_RECOVERY_GUIDE.md). Keep a clear `Pending` status rather than guessing. Stop on Git conflicts, unexpected changes, schedule ambiguity, or accidental approved-content changes.

## Monthly Use

Run the [Monthly Review](11_MONTHLY_REVIEW.md) for 30–45 minutes. Approve at most one necessary system change. VikashOS should not be redesigned every week.

## Time Protection

The system limits Vikash's weekly work by separating judgment from execution. Vikash supplies ideas, reviews truth and voice, approves publication, and interprets results. ChatGPT drafts, Codex produces and records, Supergrow scores and schedules, and Canva remains optional.
