# Leadership Journal #002

In a meeting with around 12 people, an agency representative challenged me directly.

“Sir, I don't think that's how performance marketing works.”

There are moments when your first instinct is to defend your position.

This could have been one of them.

Instead, I asked him to explain.

He had a valid point.

The moment reminded me that leadership can pull your attention in two directions.

You are responsible for the business outcome.

You also need enough attention to technical detail to recognise when the work is moving away from that outcome.

That does not mean pretending to know more than the specialist closest to the work.

It means creating enough room for that specialist to challenge you when something is wrong.

If I had reacted to the tone instead of listening to the point, the meeting would have produced less value.

Leadership is not knowing every technical answer.

It is staying accountable for the outcome while remaining willing to learn from the people who understand the detail better.

How do you respond when someone challenges your view in front of a room?
