## Final Approved LinkedIn Post

A few months ago, I asked myself a difficult question:

If the next 10 years of my career require different skills than the last 12, am I actually preparing for them?

That question stayed with me.

For years, I focused on marketing execution, campaign performance, lead generation, and business outcomes. But as I moved closer to leadership roles, I realised that being good at one function was no longer enough.

I needed to understand business more broadly.

Finance. Operations. Strategy. Technology. Leadership.

That is one reason I joined the AGMP programme at IIM Ahmedabad.

At the same time, the rise of AI changed how I looked at possibility.

I realised I did not have to remain only a user of tools.

I could start building them.

So I began learning GitHub, working with Codex, experimenting with automation, and turning ideas into practical products.

But this journey is not only about my own growth.

I have met many capable professionals and small-business owners who want to use better tools, improve their marketing, and build stronger systems, but cannot afford expensive enterprise solutions.

That gap matters to me.

My long-term mission is simple:

Making business intelligence accessible.

Through this journey, I will share what I learn about marketing, buyer behaviour, AI, business, leadership, product building, and the mistakes that come with trying something new.

I cannot promise that I will always be right.

But I can promise that I will keep learning, keep building, and share what genuinely helps.

Over the coming months, I will document the frameworks, tools, experiments, mistakes, and lessons behind this transition.

What is one skill you are deliberately building for the next stage of your career?

Learn. Build. Grow. Together.
