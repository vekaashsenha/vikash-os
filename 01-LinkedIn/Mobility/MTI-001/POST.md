# Mobility & Travel Insights #001

At a railway station, I saw a father arrive with young children and several bags.

He was already exhausted.

His cab driver had left the family at the station entrance.

From there, he had to manage the children, the luggage, and the remaining distance himself.

He would have paid ₹300 extra to be dropped closer.

Not for a better car.

Not for a more luxurious seat.

For less friction at the hardest part of the journey.

That moment stayed with me because businesses often define value through what they provide.

The customer may define it through what they no longer have to handle.

Transportation gets someone from one place to another.

Service notices where the journey is becoming difficult and removes that difficulty.

Sometimes customers pay more for comfort.

Sometimes they pay more for certainty.

And sometimes they simply pay for peace of mind when their hands are already full.

The most valuable part of a journey may be the friction the customer never has to face.

Where in your customer journey would removing one small point of friction create the most relief?
