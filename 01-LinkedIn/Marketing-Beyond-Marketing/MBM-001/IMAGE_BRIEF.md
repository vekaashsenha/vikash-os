# Static Image Brief: ROAS vs Business ROI

## Objective

Create one premium LinkedIn static image that communicates the central lesson from the approved post without needing a dashboard, photograph, or detailed explanation.

## Format

- LinkedIn portrait static image
- 1080 × 1350 px
- Mobile-first readability
- Minimal composition with generous negative space

## Main Text

**ROAS can be positive while business ROI is negative.**

## Supporting Text

Marketing performance is not automatically business performance.

## Series Label

Marketing Beyond Marketing #001

## Visual Hierarchy

1. Make the main text the dominant element.
2. Give `ROAS` and `business ROI` equal visual weight.
3. Use restrained contrast to distinguish `positive` from `negative`; do not rely on colour alone.
4. Place the supporting text below the main statement at a clearly smaller size.
5. Keep the series label quiet and secondary near the lower edge.

## Typography Direction

- Use a refined sans-serif typeface with strong legibility.
- Use weight and spacing, rather than decorative effects, to create emphasis.
- Keep line lengths short and the layout calm.
- Use sentence case.
- Limit the design to two type weights where possible.

## Colour and Composition

- Use a restrained neutral palette with one controlled accent colour.
- Prefer an editorial, text-led layout.
- Maintain strong contrast for mobile viewing.
- Use subtle rules, blocks, or spacing only if they help clarify the contrast between the two measures.

## What to Avoid

- Stock photography or staged office scenes
- People pointing at charts
- Generic dashboards, graphs, gauges, or fake data
- Money symbols, coins, arrows, rockets, trophies, or celebration imagery
- Neon gradients, glossy effects, or excessive shadows
- Red-versus-green as the only way to communicate meaning
- Additional figures, company details, dates, or claims
- Crowded layouts and long explanatory copy

## Accessibility

- Keep the main statement readable at mobile size.
- Use explicit words, not colour alone, to express positive and negative outcomes.
- Preserve sufficient contrast between text and background.

## Suggested Alt Text

Minimal text-led graphic stating that ROAS can be positive while business ROI is negative, with a supporting line that marketing performance is not automatically business performance.
