## LinkedIn Post

I once walked into a performance review feeling confident.

My campaigns were showing ROAS as high as 10x.

I expected that result to speak for itself.

Instead, I was told:

“Your ROAS may be 10x, but the business ROI is still negative.”

Then came the harder sentence:

“You understand marketing metrics, but you are not yet ready to discuss business ROI.”

Honestly, it hurt.

At first, I thought the feedback was unfair.

But later, I asked myself an uncomfortable question:

What if they were right?

ROAS told me how much revenue advertising generated compared with ad spend.

It did not tell me whether the business was actually making money.

For that, I needed to understand pricing, margins, sales conversion, operations, retention, cash flow, and the complete business process.

That conversation changed how I looked at marketing.

A campaign can show strong ROAS, low CPL, and high lead volume while the business still loses money.

Marketing performance is not automatically business performance.

That realization is also one reason I chose to strengthen my understanding of finance, operations, strategy, and leadership through the AGMP programme at IIM Ahmedabad.

Today, before celebrating a campaign result, I ask a different question:

Is this creating profitable business growth?

What is one piece of difficult feedback that changed how you approach your profession?
