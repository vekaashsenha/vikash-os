# Marketing Beyond Marketing #002

A VP Finance once asked whether marketing was producing anything.

The question caught me off guard.

We had been sharing quarterly ROI sheets.

The reports contained the numbers.

But the contribution had still not become visible to one of the people who needed to understand it.

My first instinct was to think: we already shared this.

Then I realised that sending a report and communicating impact are not the same thing.

A spreadsheet may be accurate.

It may still fail to answer the questions a decision-maker carries into the room.

What changed because of this work?

What did it contribute to the business?

Why should anyone remember it after the meeting ends?

The VP Finance was not responsible for translating a marketing report into marketing's case.

That was our responsibility.

Since then, I have looked at reporting differently.

The work is not finished when the numbers are sent.

It is finished when the business impact is clear enough to be understood and remembered.

How do you make your function's contribution visible beyond the reports you send?
