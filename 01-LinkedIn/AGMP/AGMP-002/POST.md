# AGMP With Vikash #002

Sometimes the room becomes part of the curriculum.

That has been one of my observations from the AGMP programme at IIM Ahmedabad.

The cohort includes founders, senior professionals, and people from very different backgrounds.

Each person enters the room with a different view of how business works.

The same discussion can be heard through the lens of marketing, finance, operations, technology, or entrepreneurship.

My own experience is valuable.

But it is still only one part of a much wider business world.

That is easy to forget when we spend years inside one function or industry.

We become familiar with certain problems.

We also become familiar with certain ways of solving them.

Then someone from a different background asks a question we would not have considered.

The subject may begin with the material.

The learning often becomes richer through the perspectives sitting around it.

For me, that is one of the most useful parts of learning with an experienced cohort.

The curriculum gives the room a subject.

The room gives the subject more dimensions.

When has someone from a different background changed the way you understood a business problem?
