# Video Script: The Process Behind the Promise

## Format

- First-person, direct-to-camera LinkedIn video
- 45–60 seconds
- Natural and reflective delivery
- No claims beyond the approved post

## Script

One sentence from my AGMP class at IIM Ahmedabad has stayed with me ever since:

A business promise is also an operations promise.

A business may promise fast service, personalisation, convenience, quick support, or delivery within a certain time.

Customers never experience your marketing.

They experience what happens after they buy.

If capacity is insufficient, one bottleneck slows the flow, or customers are passed between departments and forced to wait, the promise fails.

The class helped me see operations differently.

I used to think operations was simply execution.

Now I see it differently.

Operations decides whether strategy survives contact with the customer.

It is the system that determines whether the strategy can actually be delivered.

Marketing can generate demand. But if the process cannot absorb that demand, it may only expose the weakness faster.

Before making a customer promise, ask whether the operating system can honour it.

## On-Screen Text

Use no more than three text moments:

1. `A marketing promise is easy to write.`
2. `Customers experience the process behind it.`
3. `Can the operating system honour the promise?`

## Delivery Notes

- Speak as a professional sharing a considered classroom reflection.
- Pause after the opening contrast.
- Keep the pace steady and conversational.
- Avoid dramatic music, exaggerated reactions, and rapid transitions.
- Do not add professor quotations, statistics, case facts, or classroom details.
