# Carousel: Can Operations Deliver the Promise?

## Format

- 7 slides
- One clear idea per slide
- Concise, mobile-readable copy
- Professional and restrained visual treatment

## Slide 1 — Hook

**A marketing promise is easy to write.**

**An operations promise is much harder to deliver.**

## Slide 2 — The Promise

A business may promise:

Fast service.  
Personalisation.  
Convenience.  
Quick support.  
Delivery within a certain time.

## Slide 3 — The Experience

The customer does not experience the advertisement.

**The customer experiences the process behind it.**

## Slide 4 — Where Promises Fail

Insufficient capacity.

One bottleneck slowing the flow.

Customers passed between departments and forced to wait.

## Slide 5 — A Different View of Operations

Operations is not merely execution after strategy.

**It is the system that determines whether the strategy can actually be delivered.**

## Slide 6 — The Sequence

**Corporate strategy** defines the promise.

**Customers** choose based on that promise.

**Operations** designs the process, capacity, buffers, and customer flow required to fulfil it.

## Slide 7 — The Question

Before making a customer promise, ask whether the operating system can honour it.

What is one promise your business makes that depends heavily on operations to deliver?
