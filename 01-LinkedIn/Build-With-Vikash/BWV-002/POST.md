# Build With Vikash #002

A few months ago, words like push, commit, and API felt completely alien to me.

Now they are part of my everyday vocabulary.

I am not claiming to be a developer.

I am still learning what these systems expect and why they fail.

But building has changed the way I look at technical work.

The logic can feel perfectly clear in your mind.

Then it meets a working system.

Something does not connect.

A permission is wrong.

A command behaves differently than expected.

Or one small change breaks something that was working before.

That gap between intention and execution can be exhausting.

It has also given me more respect for the specialists who work through that gap every day.

From outside, we often see the finished feature.

We do not see the hours spent finding the one place where the logic stopped translating into reality.

Building has not made me a developer.

It has made me a better listener when developers explain why something that sounds simple is not simple to implement.

What has building something yourself helped you understand about another profession?
