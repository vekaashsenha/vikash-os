Three weeks ago, I thought building a product was all about having the right idea.

I couldn't have been more wrong.

Then I started building.

The idea was actually the easy part.

The real work began afterwards.

Understanding Git.

Learning GitHub.

Managing repositories.

Fixing broken workspaces.

Connecting different tools.

Writing better prompts.

Designing workflows.

Testing.

Breaking things.

Fixing them.

Then breaking something else.

The biggest surprise?

The more I build, the less I think software is about code.

It's about building systems that continue to work even when something breaks.

A small mistake in one part can stop everything.

One permission.

One missing folder.

One broken integration.

Everything stops.

That reminded me of something I've spent years learning in marketing.

A campaign is never just an advertisement.

It's a system.

Software isn't much different.

Ideas don't build products.

Systems do.

I'm still learning.

I'm still making mistakes.

But every mistake leaves behind a better system than the one I had yesterday.

Looking back, I'm glad things kept breaking.

Every problem forced me to build a better system.

That's what progress looks like.

What's one thing you thought would be the hardest part of building, only to discover the real challenge was somewhere else?
