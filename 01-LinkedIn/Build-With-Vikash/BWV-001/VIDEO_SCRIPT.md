# Video Script: Ideas Don't Build Products

## Format

- First-person, direct-to-camera LinkedIn video
- 45–60 seconds
- Natural conversational delivery
- No claims beyond the approved post

## Script

Three weeks ago, I thought the hardest part of building a product would be the idea.

Then I started building, and the idea turned out to be the easy part.

The real work was understanding Git, managing repositories, fixing broken workspaces, connecting tools, writing better prompts, designing workflows, and testing.

Then came the cycle: breaking things, fixing them, and breaking something else.

The more I build, the less I think software is only about code. It is about systems.

One permission, one missing folder, or one broken integration can stop everything.

It reminded me of marketing. A campaign is never just an advertisement. It is a system too.

I am still learning and still making mistakes.

Every mistake leaves behind a better system than the one I had yesterday.

## On-Screen Text

Use no more than three text moments:

1. `The idea was the easy part.`
2. `Software is about systems.`
3. `Ideas don't build products. Systems do.`

## Delivery Notes

- Speak as if sharing an honest build update with a colleague.
- Pause after the first two sentences.
- Keep the pace steady and conversational.
- Avoid dramatic music, exaggerated reactions, and rapid transitions.
- Do not add achievements, product outcomes, companies, or technical details beyond the approved post.
