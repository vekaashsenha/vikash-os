# Static Image Brief: Ideas and Systems

## Objective

Create one premium editorial LinkedIn image that communicates the approved post's central lesson: ideas matter, but products emerge from working systems.

## Format

- LinkedIn portrait
- 1080 × 1350 px
- Mobile-first readability
- Minimal, text-led composition

## Main Text

**Ideas don't build products.**

**Systems do.**

## Series Label

Build With Vikash #001

## Visual Hierarchy

1. Make `Ideas don't build products.` the clear opening statement.
2. Give `Systems do.` the strongest typographic emphasis.
3. Keep the series label small and secondary near the lower edge.
4. Use negative space to make the contrast feel deliberate and calm.

## Typography Direction

- Use a refined, highly legible sans-serif typeface.
- Use no more than two type weights.
- Create emphasis through scale, weight, and spacing.
- Keep line lengths short and use sentence case.
- Avoid decorative type effects.

## Colour and Composition

- Use a restrained neutral palette with one controlled accent colour.
- Prefer a premium editorial layout over a technology-themed layout.
- Use a subtle grid or connecting rule only if it supports the idea of a system.
- Maintain strong contrast for mobile viewing.

## What to Avoid

- Stock photos
- AI robots, humanoid machines, or artificial brains
- Code screens, terminal windows, keyboards, or laptop clichés
- Glowing circuits, neon gradients, or futuristic interfaces
- Light bulbs, rockets, gears, puzzle pieces, or trophy imagery
- Fake product screens, performance numbers, or achievements
- Crowded layouts and explanatory paragraphs

## Suggested Alt Text

Minimal editorial graphic with the statement “Ideas don't build products. Systems do.” displayed in a restrained, text-led layout.
