# Inside the Buying Committee

Powered by the Vikash Industry Intelligence Framework

Permanent hashtag: `#BuyerPsychologyWithVikash`

This series studies how different industries buy, who influences the decision, what each stakeholder cares about, and how those insights should shape marketing, sales, and customer conversations.

The public LinkedIn post is only the visible output. The underlying research must follow VIIF before a post is treated as ready.

## What VIIF Adds

VIIF keeps the series practical and disciplined. It helps define the industry clearly, separate broad categories, map the business model, identify the ICP, understand the buying committee, build role-based personas, and translate the research into marketing and sales implications.

Broad labels such as "IT" or "Technology" are too generic. Where relevant, research should separate categories such as IT Services, Cybersecurity, Cloud and Computing, SaaS, Enterprise Software, Managed Services, Technology Consulting, and Infrastructure Services.

## Core Questions

- Which industry is being studied, and what is excluded?
- What type of organization is the ICP?
- Who is involved in the buying committee?
- What does each stakeholder care about?
- Who has decision power, and who has influence?
- What triggers the buying process?
- What creates hesitation or internal resistance?
- What proof does each stakeholder need?
- What should marketing communicate?
- What should sales avoid?
- Which AI opportunities are realistic for this industry?

## Working Rule

Do not invent buyer behaviour, personal stories, business numbers, or industry facts. Use verified sources, real observations, sales conversations, customer feedback, public research, and clearly marked assumptions.
