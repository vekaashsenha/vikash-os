# AI Visibility Journal

## Series Purpose

AI Visibility Journal helps founders, marketers, and business leaders understand how AI is changing search, discovery, trust, and brand visibility.

The series positions Vikash as a marketing operator who is learning, researching, and building in this space. QueryCite.com is supporting evidence of that journey, but it must not dominate every post.

The series must educate before promoting.

## Target Audience

- Founders.
- Small-business owners.
- B2B marketers.
- Marketing leaders.
- SaaS teams.
- SEO professionals.
- Agencies.
- Businesses concerned about declining search traffic.
- Companies trying to understand whether AI platforms can discover and recommend them.

## Core Content Themes

- Founder conversations.
- Changes in customer search behaviour.
- Declining or shifting website traffic.
- AI search and traditional search.
- GEO and AEO education.
- AI visibility misconceptions.
- Website and brand audits.
- Building QueryCite.
- Product experiments.
- Marketing predictions.
- What businesses should do next.
- Lessons from QueryCite users, only when genuine and approved.

## Writing and Promotion Rules

1. Teach before promoting.
2. Put the story before the product.
3. Put the customer problem before the product feature.
4. Use real experiences and observations only.
5. Never invent founder conversations, customer quotations, audit results, metrics, recommendations, or product outcomes.
6. QueryCite does not need to appear in every AI Visibility Journal post.
7. When QueryCite is mentioned, connect it naturally to the problem being discussed.
8. Do not open with “Try QueryCite,” “We launched,” or generic product promotion.
9. Do not make unsupported claims that AI has replaced Google or that SEO is dead.
10. Distinguish clearly between observation, hypothesis, experiment, and confirmed evidence.
11. Do not publish confidential customer information.
12. Do not criticise founders, companies, agencies, or clients.
13. Do not exaggerate QueryCite's maturity, customers, capabilities, or results.
14. Use [Candid Operator Storytelling](../../00-Brand-Bible/CANDID_OPERATOR_STORYTELLING.md) when a genuine incident exists.
15. Mix story-led posts with analytical, educational, and practical posts.

## Story-Led Composition

When a genuine incident supports the post, use approximately:

- 70% real incident or problem.
- 20% business or marketing insight.
- 10% natural QueryCite connection.

This is guidance, not a rigid writing formula.

## Cadence and Scheduling

- AI Visibility Journal is the seventh permanent VikashOS content pillar.
- It does not automatically create a seventh weekly post.
- Maintain the current sustainable weekly publishing volume.
- Initially publish approximately once every two weeks.
- Rotate the strongest available AI Visibility Journal story into the six-post schedule.
- Replace the least relevant or weakest pillar for that particular week without permanently removing it.
- Increase frequency only after enough genuine QueryCite experiences, experiments, and evidence exist.
- Sunday is not a mandatory publishing day.
